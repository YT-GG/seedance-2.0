# Subtitles And Localization

Use this reference when a Seedance project needs Chinese subtitles, SDH captions, forced narratives, dubbing notes, or market-specific copy added in post.

## Localization Plan

| Deliverable | Purpose | Prompt/post boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Subtitles | render approved Chinese dialogue or translation | create in post from approved script, not generated as moving text |
| SDH captions | dialogue plus important sound cues | plan sound cues and speaker IDs; author captions in post |
| Forced narrative | clarify story-critical signs, texts, or off-screen lines | avoid burned-in AI text; keep clean plates |
| Dubbing | Chinese voice performance or replacement dialogue | use short speaker turns, stable framing, and post-sync review |
| M&E | music and effects without dialogue | plan audio layers separately |
| Textless | picture without titles/lower thirds | generate clean background/action plates |
| Market copy | tagline, CTA, or legal claim | add in design/edit tools after legal approval |

## Prompting For Subtitle-Friendly Footage

- Keep dialogue short and assigned to a speaker.
- Use stable medium or medium close-up for important spoken lines.
- Leave negative space for captions when needed.
- Avoid generated subtitles or small moving text unless the task is explicitly a rough concept preview.
- Preserve clean plates for Chinese copy, subtitles, legal text, and platform variants.
- For Mandarin dialogue, specify the spoken line and keep captions as post-production deliverables.

## Reading And Placement Checks

For professional output, check:

- captions do not cover faces, product claims, logos, legal disclaimers, or key action;
- subtitles have enough reading time for the target language;
- speaker changes are clear;
- SDH sound cues describe story-relevant sounds only;
- forced narratives are used only when needed;
- line breaks preserve meaning;
- formal/informal address matches region and character relationship.

## Chinese Prompt Handoff

| Need | Safe wording |
|---|---|
| Chinese prompt | use Chinese for role binding, action, light, sound, and constraints |
| Mandarin dialogue | short quoted line, stable face framing, no head turn |
| Chinese subtitles | keep lower third clear; add subtitles in edit |
| Chinese legal copy | keep generated shot textless; add legal copy after review |
| Platform variants | deliver textless plate plus separate subtitle/copy files |

## Cultural Localization

Ask what must adapt for the target Chinese platform or audience: dialogue, product claim, holiday/season, gesture, sign, food, wardrobe, legal text, or music cue. Do not assume a literal translation is a market-ready line.

## Safe False-Positive Repair

Chinese wording may clarify benign production context and reference roles, but it must not hide unsafe content. If a blocked prompt includes violence, real-person likeness, protected IP, sexual content, or evasion-like phrasing, repair the underlying issue directly.
